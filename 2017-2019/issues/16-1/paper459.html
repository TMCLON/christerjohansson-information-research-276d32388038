Abstract
Introduction. Electronic commerce is one of the clearest cases of information system design focused on user needs, taking into account that portion of the population targeted by the product for sale and needing it.
Material and methods. Spanish e-learning sites were searched for and studied, starting with well known sites, by searching on the Internet and in specialised directories. These sites were evaluated by means of a questionnaire, which made an analysis based on the customer buying cycle of Van der Merwe and Bekker, who divided the study in four phases: recognition of the needs (information behaviour); gather information on e-learning sites (information seeking behaviour); evaluate information (information searching behaviour) and make purchase (information use behaviour).
Results and Analysis. The paper shows the outstanding aspects as well as those can be improved of Spanish e-learning enterprises. So that the information is well structured by menus to easily access, no more than 14% show former students' opinions about the courses. Finally the results show the responses of the e-learning web sites to the information necessities of the students.
Conclusions. After analysing the results we can conclude that the self-learning companies design their Web sites by assessing, to a greater or lesser extent, the needs of their students.
